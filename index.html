How I'm skipping RRTK

This post is about how I study Japanese vocabulary with no prior kanji study (RRTK).

First, I'll go over some approaches I've tried and didn't like. Then, I'll explain my current method, and how it resolves the problems I had with the other approaches.

The 'vibes' approach (didn't work).

This is the first thing I thought of trying, when I had no experience with the SRS. I decided I would completely ignore the radicals. I would just look at the vague shape of the word and guess the answer. Then hope that it sticks.

My cards looked something like this.

Front
交通
Back
こ う つ う

traffic, transportation, communication, exchange (of ideas, etc.)

I very quickly realised that, without RRTK, this approach is terrible. My answers were no better than random chance. I didn't learn anything.

Incorporating RRTK stories (too hard).

Next, I decided to try incorporating RRTK. I would recall the RRTK keyword for each kanji in addition to the word itself.

My cards now looked like this.

Front
交通
Back
こ う つ う

traffic, transportation, communication, exchange (of ideas, etc.)

交   If your dad (父) is outside (wearing a hat, 亠), he will MINGLE with the neighbours.

通   Those who use (用) the road (⻌) are TRAFFIC.

This worked a lot better. Unlike the 'vibes' approach, with RRTK you actually utilise the radicals. My retention rate was high. But there was still an issue.

The reviews were hard. Recalling two RRTK keywords alongside the vocabulary is time consuming. The speed at which I could learn new words was very limited. I needed an easier approach.

Half RRTK, half vibes (just right).

This approach is a balance of the previous two, and it's what I still use to this day. I only include an RRTK story for one kanji, and I ignore the other kanji.

My cards now look like this.

Front
頻度
Back
ひ ん ど

frequency (of occurrence) e.g. weekly, daily, monthly, etc.

頻   Going for walks (歩) is good for your head (頁) if you go REPEATEDLY.

No story for 度

Ignoring one of the kanji in the word reduces the workload significantly, and I haven't seen any drop in my retention rates!

Occasionally, words will 'clash' and share the chosen kanji.

頻度   ひ ん ど
頻繁 ひ ん ぱ ん

I have these two words in my deck, and for both of them, I chose to focus on the kanji 頻. So how do I tell them apart?

This is where 'vibes' come in. 'Vibes' weren't effective for identifying the entire word. But when RRTK is applied to one kanji, it narrows down the possibilities significantly, and 'vibes' is just enough to cross the finish line.

This is how my internal dialogue might sound while reviewing these cards.

  1. I know the first kanji means 'repeatedly,' so it's either ひ ん ど or ひ ん ぱ ん (RRTK).
  2. The second kanji has loads of strokes, so... I'll say ひ ん ぱ ん (vibes).

Conclusion.

I'm really excited about this trick. Thanks to this, I've been able to skip the RRTK phase, and jump straight into vocabulary study. I hope anyone who is struggling with RRTK finds this helpful.
