How I'm skipping RRTK

This post is about how I study Japanese vocabulary with no prior kanji study (RRTK).

First, I'll go over some approaches I've tried and didn't like. Then, I'll explain my current method, and how it resolves the problems I had with the other approaches.

The 'vibes' approach (didn't work).

This is the first thing I thought of trying, when I had no experience with the SRS. I wouldn't use any conscious process. I would just look at the vague shape of the word and guess the answer, purely based on intuition. Then hope it sticks.

My cards looked something like this.

Front Back
交通
こ う つ う

traffic, transportation, communication, exchange (of ideas, etc.)

I very quickly realised that, without RRTK, this approach is terrible. My answers were no better than random chance. I didn't learn anything.

Incorporating RRTK stories (too hard).

Next, I decided to try incorporating RRTK. I would recall the RRTK keyword for each kanji in addition to the word itself.

My cards now looked like this.

Front Back
交通
こ う つ う

traffic, transportation, communication, exchange (of ideas, etc.)

交   If your dad (父) is outside (wearing a hat, 亠), he will MINGLE with the neighbours.

通   Those who use (用) the road (⻌) are TRAFFIC.

This worked a lot better. Unlike the 'vibes' approach, with RRTK you actually utilise the radicals, rather than ignoring them. I actually remembered the cards. But there was still an issue.

The reviews were hard. It is a lot of work to recall two RRTK keywords alongside the vocabulary. Especially since this approach results in doing RRTK out of order, which is even tougher.

The speed I could learn new words was very limited. I needed an easier approach.

Half RRTK, half vibes (just right).

This is the method I currently use. Simply put, I only learn one RRTK story from the word, and I leave the other kanji up to 'vibes' (effectively ignore it).

My cards now look like this.

Front Back
頻度
ひ ん ど

frequency (of occurrence) e.g. weekly, daily, monthly, etc.

頻   Going for walks (歩) is good for your head (頁) if you go REPEATEDLY.

No story for 度.

This allows for making use of the radicals without requiring the extra effort of writing and recalling two stories.

Occasionally, there is another word in the deck which shares the chosen kanji. In this case, I also have this word

頻度   ひ ん ど
頻繁 ひ ん ぱ ん

which I use the same RRTK story to memorise.

This is where 'vibes' come in. Because this is so uncommon, simply 'eyeballing' the shape of the second kanji is enough for me to tell the difference between them.

Conclusion.

I'm really excited about this trick. I think it incorporates just enough RRTK to be effective, without bogging down speed of progress.

Thanks to this, I've been able to accelerate my progress of learning to read right from my first few weeks of using Anki.
